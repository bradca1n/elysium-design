## 1. CBI Authorization for Fund Administrators

### Legal Basis

Fund administrators in Ireland require authorization from the Central Bank of Ireland (CBI) under **Section 10 of the Investment Intermediaries Act 1995** (IIA). The CBI acts as competent authority for all fund service providers domiciled in Ireland. Fund administration is classified as an investment business service under the IIA.

### Application Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Capital adequacy** | Minimum initial capital proportionate to operational risk. MiFID investment firms require EUR 125,000 minimum; fund administrators' own-funds requirement is set by the CBI based on the applicant's risk profile, typically EUR 125,000-635,000 depending on activities |
| **Business plan** | 3-year financial projections, organizational chart with reporting lines, IT infrastructure description, outsourcing arrangements, risk management framework |
| **Organizational requirements** | Adequate staffing (number and expertise), systems and controls, compliance function, internal audit (or outsourced equivalent), documented procedures for all core functions |
| **Governance** | Board composition rationale, separation of duties, compliance monitoring program, conflicts of interest policy |
| **IT infrastructure** | Systems for NAV calculation, transfer agency, regulatory reporting, data security, business continuity |
| **Outsourcing** | Full details of any outsourced functions including due diligence on delegates, contractual arrangements, oversight procedures |

### Timeline and Process

1. **Pre-application meeting** with CBI (recommended, not mandatory)
2. **Submission** of completed application form, business plan, and all supporting documentation
3. **CBI assessment** period: typically **3-6 months** from receipt of a complete application (clock stops for information requests)
4. **Fitness & Probity** assessment of all PCF holders (see Section 2)
5. **Authorization** granted with conditions (if applicable)

### Ongoing Conditions

- Maintain regulatory capital at all times
- Report changes in qualifying shareholders (10%+ holdings) to CBI in advance
- Notify CBI of material changes to business plan, outsourcing arrangements, IT systems, or governance
- Submit regulatory returns via CBI Online Reporting (ONR) system
- Annual audited financial statements filed with CBI
- Comply with all applicable CBI Codes of Conduct and guidance

## 2. CBI Fitness & Probity Regime

### Legal Basis

**Central Bank Reform Act 2010, Part 3** establishes the Fitness & Probity (F&P) regime. Updated guidance published November 2025 consolidates all F&P requirements into a single document.

### Pre-Approval Controlled Functions (PCFs)

Individuals in PCF roles require **prior CBI approval** via submission of an Individual Questionnaire (IQ). The CBI maintains approximately 57 PCF roles (as of November 2025). Key PCFs for fund administrators:

| PCF Code | Role | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| PCF-1 | CEO / Executive Director | Chief executive, managing director |
| PCF-2 | Non-Executive Director | Independent oversight role |
| PCF-3 | Chairman | Board chairperson |
| PCF-7 | COO | Chief operating officer |
| PCF-11 | Head of Finance | Financial controller / CFO |
| PCF-12 | Head of Compliance | Compliance oversight |
| PCF-13 | Head of Internal Audit | Internal audit function |
| PCF-14 | Chief Risk Officer (CRO) | Risk management oversight |
| PCF-15 | Head of AML/CFT Compliance | AML reporting officer (formerly MLRO) |
| PCF-39 (replaced) | Designated Person | Replaced by PCF-46 to PCF-51 (6 DP roles aligned to CP86 managerial functions) |
| PCF-46 | DP - Investment Management | CP86 Managerial Function 1 |
| PCF-47 | DP - Fund Risk Management | CP86 Managerial Function 2 |
| PCF-48 | DP - Operational Risk Management | CP86 Managerial Function 3 |
| PCF-49 | DP - Regulatory Compliance | CP86 Managerial Function 4 |
| PCF-50 | DP - Distribution | CP86 Managerial Function 5 |
| PCF-51 | DP - Capital & Financial Management | CP86 Managerial Function 6 |
| PCF-52 | Head of AML/CFT | Designated under Criminal Justice Act |
| PCF-56 | Head of Safeguarding (PI/EMI) | New (2025) |
| PCF-57 | Head of Safeguarding (CASP) | New (2025 -- crypto asset service providers) |

### Controlled Functions (CFs)

Broader category; individuals require **annual certification** by the regulated entity (no prior CBI approval needed):

| CF Code | Scope |
|---------|-------|
| CF-1 | Customer-facing roles |
| CF-2 | Providing advice or information |
| CF-3 | Ensuring compliance or risk management |
| CF-4 | Administrative, supervisory, or managerial roles |

### Standards

All PCF and CF holders must meet four standards at all times:
1. **Competence** -- appropriate qualifications, knowledge, and experience
2. **Honesty** -- truthfulness in all dealings with the CBI and customers
3. **Integrity** -- adherence to ethical standards
4. **Financial soundness** -- no undischarged bankruptcy or adverse financial history

### Individual Accountability Framework (IAF)

Enacted **9 March 2023** under the Central Bank (Individual Accountability Framework) Act 2023. Four pillars:

1. **SEAR (Senior Executive Accountability Regime):** Applied to in-scope firms from **1 July 2024** (credit institutions, insurance, investment firms); extended to non-executive directors **July 2025**. Fund management companies are expected to come into scope in a subsequent phase.
   - **Statements of Responsibilities** required for each PCF holder
   - **Management Responsibilities Map** for each firm
   - **Duty of Responsibility:** PCF holders must take "all reasonable steps" to prevent regulatory breaches in their areas of responsibility
2. **Common Conduct Standards:** Apply to all CF and PCF holders -- act honestly, act in customers' best interests, report concerns
3. **Additional Conduct Standards:** Apply to PCF holders -- ensure business is conducted responsibly, ensure compliance, cooperate with CBI
4. **Business Conduct Standards:** Apply to all regulated firms -- act in best interests of customers, effective governance, culture of compliance

## 3. CP86 Deep Dive

### Six Designated Person (DP) Managerial Functions

Each function must be assigned to a named individual (a DP). The same person **must not** perform both investment management (DP4) and fund risk management (DP2).

#### DP1: Regulatory & Compliance
- Regulatory filings and notifications to CBI
- AML/CFT oversight (including transaction monitoring, STR filing, sanctions screening)
- Compliance monitoring program: design, execution, and reporting
- Maintenance of regulatory registers (complaints, breaches, conflicts)
- Regulatory change management and impact assessment

#### DP2: Fund Risk Management
- Investment risk oversight: market risk, credit risk, concentration risk
- Counterparty risk monitoring and exposure limits
- Liquidity risk monitoring (ESMA LST guidelines compliance)
- Risk reporting to the board (dashboards, risk appetite breaches, stress test results)
- Oversight of risk models and methodologies

#### DP3: Operational Risk Management
- Business continuity planning (BCP) and disaster recovery
- Incident management and escalation procedures
- Outsourcing oversight: delegate due diligence, SLA monitoring, performance reviews
- Technology risk: cybersecurity, system resilience, DORA compliance
- Key person risk, change management, and operational loss tracking

#### DP4: Investment Management
- Investment mandate monitoring: compliance with prospectus restrictions
- Portfolio management oversight: best execution, trade allocation, soft commissions
- Pre-trade and post-trade compliance checks
- Oversight of delegated investment managers (where portfolio management is outsourced)
- Performance attribution and benchmark monitoring

#### DP5: Capital & Financial Management
- Regulatory capital adequacy: maintain minimum own funds at all times
- Financial reporting: management accounts, statutory financial statements
- Budgeting and expense monitoring
- Pricing oversight: NAV calculation governance, pricing committee support
- Tax compliance for the management company entity

#### DP6: Distribution
- Distribution oversight: distributor due diligence, agreement management
- Investor suitability and appropriateness (MiFID II obligations where applicable)
- Commission and rebate management: retrocession agreements, inducements compliance
- Marketing material approval and regulatory review
- Investor complaints handling within distribution channel

### Organisational Effectiveness Role

- Assigned to an **independent board member** who does not hold a DP function
- Keeps the organizational arrangements under **continuous review**
- Monitors conflicts of interest across the fund management company
- Oversees effectiveness of internal audit (unless allocated to a specific DP)
- **Reports annually** to the board on organizational effectiveness findings and recommendations

### Substance Requirements

| Requirement | CBI Expectation |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Director time commitment** | CBI typically expects **20-30 days/year** for independent non-executive directors; more for directors holding DP roles |
| **Irish-resident directors** | Minimum **2-3 Irish-resident directors** (requirement varies by entity type and complexity) |
| **Conducting officers** | Minimum **2 conducting officers** based in Ireland |
| **Record retrievability** | Same-day retrieval for requests received before **1:00 PM**; next business day for requests received after 1:00 PM |
| **Board meetings** | Minimum quarterly (CBI expectation); monthly for larger ManCos |
| **Annual CP86 effectiveness review** | Self-assessment questionnaire, board discussion, documented action items |

### Managerial Functions Regulations

- Documented delegation framework: which functions are delegated to whom, with what oversight
- DPs must have **direct access** to the board
- Each DP function must have adequate resources, systems, and support
- CBI may interview DPs as part of supervisory engagement

## 4. CBI Supervisory Approach

### PRISM (Probability Risk and Impact SysteM)

Impact-based engagement model that categorizes all regulated entities by systemic importance and risk profile.

| Category | Engagement Level | Typical Entities |
|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| **High** | Continuous direct supervision, dedicated supervisor, annual risk assessment | Systemically important banks, largest insurers |
| **Medium-High** | Regular supervisory engagement, periodic on-site inspections | Large fund administrators, significant ManCos |
| **Medium-Low** | Risk-based engagement, thematic inspections | Mid-size fund administrators |
| **Low** | Reactive supervision, thematic reviews | Smaller regulated entities |

**Note (2025 reform):** The CBI introduced a new supervisory approach in February 2025 integrating conduct, integrity, and prudential supervision into multi-disciplinary teams. Medium-High and below entities no longer have dedicated direct supervision but are covered through thematic and sectoral engagement.

### Thematic Inspections (Recent Themes)

| Year | Theme | Focus |
|------|-------|-------|
| 2023 | Outsourcing oversight | Delegate governance, SLA monitoring, fourth-party risk |
| 2023 | Fund management company effectiveness | Dear CEO letter on CP86 expectations |
| 2024 | Operational resilience / DORA readiness | ICT risk management, incident reporting, testing |
| 2024-2025 | ESG integration and SFDR compliance | Greenwashing, PAI data quality, naming conventions |
| 2025 | Compliance and internal audit | ESMA Common Supervisory Action |
| Ongoing | Cyber risk | Penetration testing, data breach preparedness |

### Dear CEO Letters

Periodic industry communications setting CBI expectations. Examples:
- **2023:** Fund management company effectiveness -- reminded boards of CP86 obligations, governance culture, and substance expectations
- **2024:** Operational resilience -- DORA readiness, third-party ICT risk management

### CBI Online Reporting (ONR)

- **Quarterly/annual regulatory returns** filed electronically
- Fund-level and entity-level data
- AML returns, financial reporting, statistical returns

### Administrative Sanctions Procedure (ASP)

Updated guidelines effective **13 December 2023**. Process:

1. **Investigation:** CBI Enforcement team investigates suspected prescribed contravention; Responsible Authorised Officer (RAO) appointed
2. **Settlement discussions:** Three types -- undisputed facts settlement, investigation report settlement, no admissions settlement
3. **Inquiry:** If no settlement reached, CBI holds a (typically public) inquiry before an Inquiry Member
4. **Sanctions:** Maximum fines up to **EUR 10 million or 10% of turnover** (whichever is greater) for regulated entities; up to **EUR 1 million** for individuals
5. **Publication:** CBI publishes settlement outcomes and inquiry decisions (public censure)

## 5. Irish Fund Structures in Depth

### ICAV (Irish Collective Asset-management Vehicle) -- ICAV Act 2015

The dominant structure for new Irish fund launches since 2015:

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Legal personality** | Yes -- body corporate with limited liability |
| **AGM requirement** | **No** (unlike PLCs -- a key advantage) |
| **Constitutional document** | Instrument of incorporation (not memorandum and articles of association) |
| **US tax treatment** | Eligible for **check-the-box** election (can elect partnership treatment for US tax purposes) |
| **Conversion** | PLCs and unit trusts can convert to ICAV under the ICAV Act |
| **Umbrella capability** | Yes -- segregated sub-funds with statutory ring-fencing |
| **Board** | Minimum 2 directors (CBI expects 3+ for ManCos) |

### QIAIF (Qualifying Investor Alternative Investment Fund)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Minimum investment** | EUR 100,000 per investor |
| **Investment restrictions** | Virtually none -- governed by prospectus (no UCITS-style diversification) |
| **CBI prior approval** | **Not required** for investment policy changes |
| **Filing** | **24-hour filing** with CBI (fast-track authorization) |
| **Eligible investors** | Qualifying investors (institutions and high-net-worth individuals meeting statutory criteria) |
| **Borrowing** | No statutory limit (per prospectus) |

### RIAIF (Retail Investor Alternative Investment Fund)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Minimum investment** | No minimum |
| **Investment restrictions** | Full UCITS-like restrictions apply (diversification, eligible assets, leverage limits) |
| **CBI prior approval** | **Required** for material changes to investment policy |
| **Borrowing** | Maximum 25% of NAV |
| **Investor access** | Retail and professional investors |

### CCF (Common Contractual Fund)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Legal personality** | **No** -- co-ownership structure |
| **Tax transparency** | Yes -- investors are treated as directly owning a proportionate share of the assets |
| **Typical use** | Institutional investors (pension funds, sovereign wealth funds) seeking treaty access |
| **Management** | Managed by a management company; no board of its own |
| **Eligible investors** | Institutional and qualifying investors only |

### ILP (Investment Limited Partnership)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Legal personality** | No -- partnership |
| **Tax transparency** | Yes -- income and gains flow through to partners |
| **Reform** | Investment Limited Partnerships (Amendment) Act 2020, effective February 2021 |
| **Safe harbor** | Statutory safe harbor provisions for LP involvement in partnership governance without losing limited liability |
| **GP residency** | **No requirement** for Irish-resident GP |
| **Typical use** | Closed-ended alternative funds (PE, VC, real estate, infrastructure) |

## 6. Cayman-Specific Requirements

### CIMA Fund Annual Return (FAR)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Filing deadline** | Within **6 months** of financial year-end |
| **Content** | Fund details (name, type, structure), NAV, AUM, investor count, service provider details, financial highlights |
| **Audited financials** | Must accompany FAR; auditor must be approved by CIMA |
| **Applicable to** | All registered mutual funds and private funds |
| **Acceptable GAAP** | IFRS, US GAAP, Japan GAAP, Swiss GAAP, or GAAP of a non-high-risk jurisdiction |
| **Annual fees** | Calculated by reference to NAV bands; paid concurrently with FAR filing |

### Directors Registration and Licensing Law (DRLL)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Registration** | All directors of CIMA-regulated mutual funds (exempted companies), managers of LLCs, and directors of GPs of CIMA-regulated ELPs must register with CIMA |
| **Professional directors** | Individuals acting as director for 20+ entities require a **CIMA license** |
| **Annual fee** | Required per registered director |
| **Private funds exception** | Directors of closed-ended private funds under the Private Funds Law are currently **not required** to register under the DRLL |
| **Ongoing obligations** | Notify CIMA of changes to registered information |

### Confidential Information Disclosure Law 2016 (CID Law)

Replaced the Confidential Relationships (Preservation) Law (CRPL) on 22 July 2016:

| Aspect | CID Law (Current) | CRPL (Repealed) |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Criminal sanctions** | **Removed** for breach of confidence | Criminal offense to disclose confidential information |
| **Gateways for disclosure** | Statutory safe harbors for lawful disclosure (regulatory requests, court orders, tax information exchange, AML obligations) | Limited exceptions only (consent, court order, normal course of business) |
| **Scope** | Applies to all confidential information received in a professional capacity in the Cayman Islands | Same |
| **Enforcement** | Civil remedies (injunctions, damages) | Criminal penalties (fines, imprisonment, disgorgement) |

### CIMA Inspection Regime

- CIMA may conduct **on-site inspections** of registered/licensed funds and their service providers
- May request production of **any documents**, books, or records
- May **interview** directors, officers, and service providers
- Inspections may be routine (scheduled) or triggered by specific concerns
- Non-cooperation is a criminal offense under the Monetary Authority Act

### Cayman AML Regulations (Anti-Money Laundering Regulations 2020)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Risk-based approach** | Funds must adopt a risk-based AML framework proportionate to size and complexity |
| **CDD** | Customer due diligence on all investors; enhanced due diligence for high-risk relationships (PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions) |
| **STR filing** | Suspicious transaction reports filed with Cayman **Financial Reporting Authority (FRA)** |
| **Record retention** | **5 years** minimum after end of business relationship |
| **AML Compliance Officer** | Must appoint AMLCO, MLRO, and Deputy MLRO |
| **Wire transfer rules** | Originator and beneficiary information must accompany wire transfers |
| **Training** | Ongoing AML training for all relevant employees |
| **Applicable law** | Proceeds of Crime Act, Anti-Money Laundering Regulations 2020, Terrorism Act |

### Beneficial Ownership Regime

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Legal basis** | Beneficial Ownership Transparency Act 2023 (replacing the 2017 regime) |
| **Register** | Legal persons must maintain a register of beneficial owners (25%+ ownership/control threshold) |
| **CIMA-regulated funds** | Mutual funds and private funds registered with CIMA are **exempt** from maintaining a beneficial ownership register, but must appoint a **Contact Person** able to provide details of the fund's Relevant Beneficial Owners (RBOs) to CIMA upon request |
| **Corporate service providers** | CSPs must maintain and file beneficial ownership information with the competent authority |
| **Access** | Cayman competent authority, tax authorities (via AEOI), law enforcement (upon request) |

## 7. Ireland vs. Cayman Comparison

| Feature | Ireland (CBI) | Cayman (CIMA) |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| **Administrator authorization** | IIA Section 10; 3-6 month process | No separate fund admin licensing regime; fund-level registration |
| **Fitness & Probity** | PCF/CF regime; IQ submission; annual certification; IAF/SEAR | DRLL registration; professional director licensing |
| **Governance framework** | CP86 (6 DP functions + OER); prescriptive, 141 pages | Corporate Governance Rule (Oct 2023); principles-based, proportionality |
| **Supervisory model** | PRISM (impact-based); thematic inspections; Dear CEO letters | Risk-based; on-site inspections; fund registration review |
| **Enforcement** | ASP: EUR 10M / 10% turnover fines; public censure | CIMA enforcement: fines, license revocation, winding-up orders |
| **Accountability** | IAF/SEAR: individual duty of responsibility; EUR 1M individual fines | No equivalent individual accountability regime |
| **Fund structures** | ICAV, VCC, CCF, ILP, Unit Trust | Exempted company, SPC, ELP, LLC, Unit Trust |
| **AML regime** | EU 6th AMLD; CBI AML Guidelines; FIU reporting | Cayman AML Regulations 2020; FRA reporting; FATF-compliant |
| **Confidentiality** | GDPR; no blanket confidentiality law | CID Law 2016; statutory safe harbors |
| **BO reporting** | EU UBO register (5th AMLD); RBO accessible to public (under challenge) | BOT Act 2023; CIMA-regulated funds exempt from register but must appoint Contact Person |
| **Record retrieval** | Same-day (pre-1pm) / next business day (post-1pm) | No prescriptive timeline; must be available on CIMA request |
| **Tax on fund** | 0% for non-Irish investors | 0% (tax-neutral jurisdiction) |

## Elysium Platform Relevance

Elysium's MVP targets Irish-licensed fund administrators servicing Cayman-domiciled funds. This file documents the dual regulatory burden the platform must support:

- **CP86 compliance features:** DP function assignment tracking, record retrievability SLAs, board meeting and effectiveness review scheduling
- **CIMA compliance features:** FAR data collection, DRLL registration tracking, AML/CDD workflow for Cayman fund investors
- **Cross-jurisdiction reporting:** CBI ONR returns (Ireland entity-level) and CIMA FAR (Cayman fund-level) from the same platform
- **Fitness & Probity tracking:** PCF/CF holder register, IQ submission status, annual certification workflow
- **IAF/SEAR readiness:** Statements of Responsibilities, Management Responsibilities Map (when fund ManCos come into SEAR scope)
