### 1.2 Statement of Financial Position (IFRS)

Under IFRS, the equivalent is the Statement of Financial Position. Key structural differences: puttable shares (redeemable at NAV) are classified as financial liabilities under IAS 32 unless the narrow equity exception applies. This means "net assets attributable to holders of redeemable participating shares" appears as a liability, and the equity section may show only management shares.

### 1.4 Statement of Changes in Net Assets

Covers two periods (current and prior year). Key line items:

- Net increase/decrease from operations (from Statement of Operations)
- Distributions to shareholders (from net investment income; from net realized gains; return of capital)
- Capital share transactions: proceeds from shares sold, reinvestment of distributions, cost of shares redeemed, net increase/decrease from share transactions
- **Net increase/decrease in net assets**
- Net assets at beginning of period / end of period

### 1.7 Statement of Cash Flows

Under US GAAP, investment companies following ASC 946 are **not required** to present a Statement of Cash Flows (ASC 230 scope exception). Under IFRS, IAS 7 applies to **all entities** including investment entities — no scope exemption exists. However, where substantially all investments are measured at fair value, the cash flow statement adds limited incremental information. Many IFRS fund financial statements present the statement using the indirect method, with investing cash flows dominated by purchases and sales of investments.

## 2. Notes to Financial Statements

### 2.1 Significant Accounting Policies

Required disclosures include: basis of preparation (IFRS or US GAAP), functional and presentation currency, fair value measurement policy, revenue recognition (dividends on ex-date, interest on accrual basis using effective interest method), foreign currency translation methodology (IAS 21 / ASC 830), use of estimates and judgments, new standards adopted.

### 2.2 Fair Value Hierarchy (IFRS 13 / ASC 820)

Three-level hierarchy with required movement table for Level 3:

| | Level 1 ($m) | Level 2 ($m) | Level 3 ($m) | Total ($m) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Equities | 285.4 | 12.8 | 4.2 | 302.4 |
| Fixed income | — | 148.6 | 8.5 | 157.1 |
| Derivatives — assets | — | 3.2 | — | 3.2 |
| Derivatives — liabilities | — | (1.8) | — | (1.8) |

**Level 3 reconciliation (roll-forward):** opening balance, total gains/losses in P&L, purchases, sales, transfers in/out, closing balance. Sensitivity analysis on unobservable inputs required (e.g., +/-10% on discount rate showing impact on fair value).

### 2.3 Risk Disclosures (IFRS 7 / Regulation S-X)

**Market risk:** Sensitivity analysis showing impact of reasonably possible changes — e.g., +/-5% equity markets = +/-$15.1m on NAV; +/-100bps interest rates = +/-$4.2m; +/-5% FX rates = +/-$3.8m.

**Credit risk:** Counterparty exposure by credit rating (S&P/Moody's), maximum exposure, collateral held. For OTC derivatives: ISDA master agreements, CSA terms, netting arrangements.

**Liquidity risk:** Maturity analysis of financial liabilities by contractual maturity bucket:

| | < 1 month | 1-3 months | 3-12 months | > 1 year |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Redeemable participating shares | 95% | 3% | 2% | — |
| Payables | 100% | — | — | — |
| Derivative liabilities | 20% | 45% | 35% | — |

**Concentration risk:** Top 10 holdings as percentage of NAV, geographic concentration, sector breakdown, single-issuer limits.

### 2.4 Related Party Transactions

Management fees (rate, basis, amount paid/accrued), performance fees, directors' fees and shareholdings, investment manager's interests in the fund, transactions with affiliated funds, seed capital, soft commissions.

### 2.5 Other Required Notes

- **Subsequent events:** Material redemptions, market events, regulatory changes between year-end and report signing date (IAS 10 / ASC 855)
- **Commitments and contingencies:** Unfunded commitments (PE/credit), pending litigation, indemnification provisions
- **Derivative positions:** Notional amounts, fair values, maturity profiles, hedge effectiveness (if hedge accounting applied under IFRS 9/ASC 815)
- **Securities lending (SFTR Article 13):** Total value of securities on loan, collateral received (type, quality, maturity), counterparties, return and cost breakdown, ten largest collateral issuers, top ten counterparties by value. Required in UCITS/AIF semi-annual and annual reports since January 2017.

## 3. Audit Process — Administrator's Perspective

### 3.1 PBC (Prepared By Client) List

The administrator prepares the following for the auditor:

| Category | Items |
|----------|-------|
| **Trial balance** | Year-end trial balance, chart of accounts mapping, general ledger detail for all accounts |
| **Financial statements** | Draft financial statements, notes, schedules (admin prepares; auditor opines) |
| **NAV packages** | Daily/weekly NAV packs for sampling, NAV reconciliation to investor statements |
| **Investment portfolio** | Schedule of investments at year-end, cost lots, unrealized gain/loss, pricing sources |
| **Confirmations** | Bank balance confirmations, custodian/prime broker confirmations (positions + cash), counterparty confirmations (OTC derivatives), transfer agent register |
| **Transaction support** | Trade blotters, settlement confirmations, corporate action processing records |
| **Income** | Dividend/interest accrual schedules, withholding tax reclaim tracking, securities lending income |
| **Expenses** | Fee accrual calculations (management, performance, admin, depositary), invoice support, expense allocation methodology for multi-class funds |
| **Investor records** | Shareholder register, subscription/redemption activity, equalization balances, distribution calculations |
| **Compliance** | Investment restriction monitoring reports, breach logs, AML documentation |
| **Pricing** | Pricing source hierarchy documentation, stale price exception log, fair value committee minutes |
| **Reconciliations** | Bank, custodian, position, and cash reconciliation reports with aged breaks |

### 3.2 Third-Party Confirmations (ISA 505 / AU-C 505)

The auditor sends confirmation requests directly to:

- **Custodian/prime broker:** Positions held (ISIN, quantity, value), cash balances, margin, collateral. Positive confirmation required.
- **Banks:** All cash accounts including deposit accounts, overdraft facilities, encumbrances
- **OTC counterparties:** Derivative positions (notional, fair value, collateral exchanged)
- **Transfer agent (if separate):** Shares outstanding by class, reconciled to register

### 3.3 Common Audit Findings

| Finding | Root Cause | Admin Impact |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| Incorrect fair value hierarchy classification | Level 1 vs Level 2 boundary unclear for thinly traded securities | Reclassify; update pricing policy documentation |
| Revenue recognition timing | Dividends accrued on pay date instead of ex-date | Restate income; correct accruals |
| Stale pricing not documented | Missing evidence of price challenge/override process | Remediate pricing exception log; update SOPs |
| Related party disclosures incomplete | Manager's interests or affiliated transactions omitted | Complete note; enhance related-party identification process |
| Expense allocation undocumented | Multi-class cost allocation methodology not formalized | Document allocation methodology in board-approved policy |
| FX translation errors | Inconsistent rate sources or timing | Standardize FX rate capture (WM/Reuters 4pm London fix) |
| Unreconciled custodian breaks | Aged position or cash differences | Resolve breaks; document rationale for any remaining items |

### 3.4 Management Representation Letter

Signed by fund directors (not the administrator). Contents include: responsibility for financial statements, completeness of information provided, no fraud or irregularities, compliance with laws and regulations, subsequent events disclosure, going concern assessment, related party completeness, no unrecorded liabilities. The admin typically prepares the draft for board review.

### 3.5 Going Concern Assessment Triggers

- Redemption pressure exceeding available liquidity (net redemptions > 20% of NAV)
- Sustained NAV decline or significant underperformance vs benchmark
- Regulatory sanction or investigation affecting operations
- Loss of key service provider (investment manager, administrator, depositary)
- Breach of minimum fund size thresholds (some prospectuses stipulate wind-down triggers)
- Inability to meet operating expenses from management fee income

### 3.6 Audit Timeline

| Phase | Timing | Activities |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Planning** | Q3 (3-4 months before YE) | Risk assessment, materiality setting, audit strategy, PBC list issued |
| **Interim testing** | Q4 (1-2 months before YE) | Controls testing (ISAE 3402 reliance), walk-throughs, transaction sampling |
| **Final fieldwork** | T+4 to T+10 weeks post YE | Substantive testing, confirmations, cut-off testing, analytical review |
| **Draft review** | T+10 to T+14 weeks | Review of draft FS, clearance of audit queries, subsequent events review |
| **Sign-off** | Within regulatory deadline | Auditor's report issued; board approves and signs financial statements |

## 4. Semi-Annual and Interim Reports

### 4.1 Content by Regulation

| Element | UCITS (EU) | AIFMD (EU) | US 1940 Act |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Balance sheet | Condensed | Similar to annual | Full |
| Income statement | Condensed | Condensed | Full |
| Schedule of investments | Changes in composition (material acquisitions/disposals) | Changes in composition | Full schedule of investments required |
| Notes | Abbreviated — significant changes only | Abbreviated | Abbreviated |
| Audit requirement | **Unaudited** (review not required) | **Unaudited** | **Unaudited** (but filed with SEC) |
| Director's report | Abbreviated | Abbreviated | Not required |
| Performance data | Yes — per-class | Yes | Yes |
| SFDR periodic disclosure | Required for Article 8/9 funds | Required for Article 8/9 funds | N/A |

### 4.2 Key Differences from Annual

- No auditor's report or opinion
- Condensed notes — only material changes to accounting policies or events since the annual report
- No full fair value hierarchy Level 3 roll-forward (though Level 3 disclosure may still be expected)
- Director's report covers only the half-year period with forward-looking commentary
- No depositary report required at interim

## 5. Annual Report Content

### 5.1 Director's Report

Strategy review, principal risks and uncertainties, significant events during the year, directors' interests in the fund, post-balance-sheet events, going concern statement, corporate governance statement (Irish Corporate Governance Code for listed funds), statement of directors' responsibilities.

### 5.2 Investment Manager's Report

Performance commentary against benchmark, market environment overview, portfolio activity (significant purchases/sales), asset allocation changes, outlook and strategy for coming period. Presented per sub-fund for umbrella structures.

### 5.3 Depositary Report (UCITS V / AIFMD)

Required by law. Depositary provides an independent opinion on:

- **Safekeeping:** All assets held in custody or verified as owned by the fund
- **Cash flow monitoring:** Subscription/redemption proceeds, investment proceeds, and income properly reconciled
- **Oversight:** NAV calculations, share dealing, income allocation, and regulatory compliance verified
- Statement that nothing has come to the depositary's attention indicating material breach

### 5.4 Remuneration Disclosure

**AIFMD Article 22(2)(e)-(f):** Total remuneration paid by the AIFM split into fixed and variable, number of beneficiaries. Aggregate remuneration broken down by senior management and identified staff. Includes delegate staff where portfolio/risk management is delegated.

**UCITS V:** Equivalent disclosure requirements for UCITS management companies. Remuneration policy summary, quantitative data for identified staff categories.

### 5.5 SFDR Periodic Disclosure

- **Article 8 funds:** Annex IV template — extent to which E/S characteristics were promoted, proportion of sustainable investments, PAI indicators considered, comparison against prior period
- **Article 9 funds:** Annex V template — attainment of sustainable investment objective, impact metrics, do-no-significant-harm assessment
- Templates annexed to the annual report (not integrated into financial statements)

### 5.6 TCFD / Climate Disclosures

Voluntary but increasingly expected by institutional investors and some regulators. Covers governance, strategy, risk management, and metrics/targets related to climate risk. The EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) indirectly increases pressure on fund managers through investee company disclosures.

## 6. Filing and Distribution Deadlines

| Jurisdiction / Regulation | Report Type | Deadline |
|---------------------------|-------------|----------|
| **UCITS (EU)** | Annual audited report | 4 months after financial year-end |
| **UCITS (EU)** | Semi-annual report | 2 months after half-year-end |
| **Irish QIAIF** | Annual audited report | 6 months after financial year-end |
| **AIFMD (EU)** | Annual report to investors | 6 months after financial year-end |
| **Cayman registered fund (CIMA)** | Audited financial statements + Fund Annual Return (FAR) | 6 months after financial year-end (extension up to 3 additional months on application) |
| **US 1940 Act (SEC)** | Annual report to shareholders | 60 days after fiscal year-end |
| **US 1940 Act (SEC)** | Semi-annual report to shareholders | 60 days after fiscal half-year-end |
| **SEC Form N-CSR** | Certified shareholder report filing | 10 days after transmission of annual/semi-annual report to shareholders |
| **CBI (Ireland)** | AIFMD Annex IV reporting | Within 30 days of quarter-end (quarterly filers) or within 30 days of half-year/year-end |
| **CRO (Ireland)** | Annual return with financial statements | 11 months after financial year-end (Companies Act 2014) |

### Distribution Methods

- Fund website (UCITS KIDs and reports must be freely available)
- Registered post for certain jurisdictions requiring physical delivery
- Electronic delivery with investor opt-in (Rule 30e-3 for US funds permits website access with notice)
- Bloomberg terminal / data vendors for institutional distribution
- Platform distributor portals (Allfunds, Clearstream Vestima)
