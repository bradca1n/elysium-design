Jargon Buster
Plain-English explanations of investing terms
ETF
A basket of many stocks bundled together. Less risky than buying a single stock because your money is spread out.
Dividend
Cash a company pays to shareholders — like rent from owning part of a business. Usually paid quarterly.
Market Cap
Total value of all a company's shares. >$200B is "mega cap" (very large, usually safer).
Volatility
How much a price swings up and down. High volatility = bigger potential gains but also bigger drops.
P/E Ratio
Price divided by yearly earnings. If P/E is 25, you're paying $25 for every $1 the company earns.
Bull / Bear Market
Bull = prices rising (optimistic). Bear = prices falling (pessimistic). Named after how each animal attacks.
